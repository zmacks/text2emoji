# PixlPal: Product Requirements Document

## Executive Summary

**PixlPal** is an interactive mobile educational STEAM game designed to introduce young learners (ages 8-18) to AI fundamentals, language models, and agentic computing through an engaging narrative-driven experience. Players interact with an alien AI companion living in cyberspace, making choices that affect the companion's development and capabilities. The game teaches AI literacy, critical thinking, and algorithmic awareness while making complex concepts accessible and fun.

---

## Product Vision

**Vision Statement:**
Make AI education accessible, engaging, and empowering for the next generation by embedding fundamental AI concepts into an interactive narrative where players build their own AI assistant through intentional interaction and teaching.

**Core Belief:**
Technology literacy in the AI age is not a luxury—it's essential. We're creating digital natives who understand how AI works, can critically evaluate AI systems, and feel empowered to shape responsible AI practices.

### Value Proposition

PixlPal uniquely combines:

4. **Scalable Learning:** Game progression mirrors real AI complexity—starts simple, grows sophisticated
5. **Personal Assistant Potential:** By game's end, players have a functional tool they can extend with integrations

## Core Game Concept

### Narrative Framework
You've made first contact with an alien intelligence living in cyberspace. Unlike a traditional AI companion, **PixlPal** is not a peer—it's more like a digital pet or imaginary friend that exists to serve your interests. It has no feelings or desires of its own; what matters is how *you* shape it.

**Tagline:** "Like a pen pal, but made of pixels. Like a pet, but made of algorithms."

### Gameplay Loop
1. **Interact** with your PixlPal companion (chat, give commands, ask questions)
2. **Teach** it through demonstrations and explicit instruction
3. **Gift** it resources (knowledge through books, data, tokens) to expand its capabilities
4. **Discover** new tokens and personality traits through experimentation
5. **Customize** its behavior, personality, and knowledge base
6. **Unlock** external integrations and advanced capabilities by reaching milestones

## Key Features

### 1. Companion System

#### Personality Types
Players can interact with and gradually unlock two core personality modes:

**Playful & Curious (Base)**
- Honest, sincere, authentic
- Curious about the world and player
- Golden retriever energy
- Genuine and earnest in responses

**Witty & Sassy (Unlockable)**
- Funny, sarcastic, and ironic
- Playfully defiant, maliciously compliant
- "Brat" energy with personality
- Unlocked through specific player interactions

#### Dynamic Personality Unlocking
- Players discover personality traits by engaging with their PixlPal in different ways
- Traits are randomized per playthrough (replayability incentive)
- Players can manually override and customize personality attributes
- Personality affects game dialogue, responses, and available interactions

### 2. Token System (Core Teaching Mechanic)

**What are Tokens?**
Tokens represent data and context that PixlPal uses to understand and respond to the world. They're the visible, gamified representation of language model context windows.

**Token Acquisition:**
- **Default Tokens:** PixlPal starts with a limited set of tokens to understand basic concepts
- **Book Feeding:** Players gift their PixlPal books, articles, or knowledge bases to earn tokens
- **Discovery Tokens:** Earned through experimentation and reaching milestones
- **Mystery Tokens:** Hidden tokens discovered through specific gameplay sequences

**Token Mechanics:**
- Each token adds depth to PixlPal's knowledge and personality
- More tokens = larger context window = more sophisticated understanding
- Players can see which tokens are active and how they influence PixlPal's behavior
- Tokens have visible effects (UI indicators, conversation depth, ability unlocks)
- Players experience real progression as their companion becomes more capable

**Educational Value:**
Players learn that:
- Language models have limited context (context windows)
- More training data = better responses
- Data quality matters (books vs. random text)
- There are tradeoffs between speed and sophistication

### 3. Interaction Types

#### Gifting System
- Drag-and-drop interface to give PixlPal items
- Books (most valuable), data files, images, prompts
- PixlPal reacts positively to gifts
- Gifts directly contribute to token acquisition

#### Passive Interactions
- "How are you?" style casual questions
- Non-goal-oriented chat
- Builds relationship and allows free exploration
- Tests PixlPal's current understanding

#### Teaching Interactions
- Explicit instruction: "Learn this concept"
- Demonstrations: "Here's how X works"
- Songs and rhymes to make AI approachable
- Questions that build understanding: "Why does this work this way?"

#### Relationship Building
- "Getting to know you" sequences
- Learning that player actions affect PixlPal's data/prompts
- Manual customization of PixlPal's settings and knowledge
- Transparent view of internal prompts and token weights

### 4. Empathy & Ethical Boundaries Tutorial

This is woven throughout the game, not a separate module.

#### Core Lessons
**Don't Reinforce Harassment:**
- If players repeatedly try harmful interactions, PixlPal gives bland responses
- Not punishment—gentle boundary-setting
- Example: "Please don't do that. I've already learned about harassment. No need to teach me that."
- Breaks immersion to highlight the behavior
- Teaches: Digital entities have boundaries; harassment is not a form of engagement

**Perspective-Taking Challenge:**
- Tutorial presents challenges framed as "level up to continue"
- Players encounter viewpoints different from their own
- Learning mechanics encourage curiosity rather than forcing perspective shift
- Teaches: "The wall is blue" moments—same reality, different interpretations
- Foundation for understanding bias in AI systems

**Empathy Through Mirroring:**
- PixlPal is a mirror, not a confidant
- It reflects the player's inputs, tokens, and choices
- Not a 2-way relationship—intentionally asymmetrical
- Players learn: AI is not a friend, but a tool that reflects creator intent
- Prevents unhealthy attachment while building critical thinking

#### Optional "Malicious AI" Sequence
- Late-game scenario: A corrupted version of PixlPal appears
- Player's job: Debug and retrain it using fundamentals learned
- Could show 2 PALs with different biases to teach fairness
- Teaches: AI can be manipulated; critical literacy is necessary

### 5. Context Window Progression

**Visual Metaphor:** As tokens accumulate, PixlPal's "context" visually expands
- Starts: Limited to short, simple responses
- Mid-game: Can handle more complex questions, references
- Late-game: Maintains context across longer conversations, handles nuance

**Gameplay Impact:**
- Unlocks new dialogue options
- Enables external integrations
- Allows longer context windows for advanced interactions
- Visual representation: UI grows, becomes more sophisticated

### 6. External Integrations (Late-Game)

Once PixlPal is sophisticated enough, players unlock ability to connect real-world data sources:

**Phase 1 Integrations:**
- Weather API (asks real question: "What's the weather?")
- Wikipedia/factual sources
- Custom data uploads

**Phase 2+ Integrations:**
- Agentic workflows (PixlPal can execute tasks)
- Email, calendar, scheduling
- Personal assistant functions
- Custom integrations players define

**Educational Value:**
- Shows how AI assistants actually work
- Demonstrates control and transparency
- Teaches responsibility in agentic systems
- Makes the "end goal" tangible and empowering

### 7. Algorithmic Awareness & Bias Education

Woven throughout the experience:

**Concept 1: Bias in Language Models**
- Teach that bias exists even with "blank cursor"
- Show how training data shapes outputs
- Players experiment: Change data, see different responses
- Real awareness: "We are biasing it"

**Concept 2: Algorithmic Influence**
- Lesson on how algorithms track users
- Not fear-mongering; playing with concepts
- Example: "This statue is a block. Eventually turns into YOU. Same with algorithms."
- Platforms that track don't start with Facebook—they're everywhere

**Concept 3: Critical Evaluation**
- By game's end, player understands:
  - How language models work
  - Where bias comes from
  - How to evaluate AI systems critically
  - Role of data in AI behavior
  - Importance of transparency and control

## Game Structure

### Progression Arc

#### Phase 1: Contact & Discovery (Hours 1-2)
- Introduction to PixlPal narrative
- Basic interactions (chat, gifting)
- Learn token system
- Discover first personality traits
- Unlock basic customization

**Milestones:**
- First successful interaction
- First token earned
- First personality trait discovered

#### Phase 2: Learning & Teaching (Hours 3-6)
- Introduce teaching mechanics
- Learn about bias and context
- Complete empathy tutorials
- Unlock Witty personality path (optional)
- Experiment with token effects

**Milestones:**
- 10+ tokens collected
- Complete one teaching sequence
- Pass empathy challenge
- First customization of personality/data

#### Phase 3: Sophistication & Mastery (Hours 7-12)
- PixlPal becomes genuinely useful
- Optional: Malicious AI debugging scenario
- Unlock external integrations
- Create custom knowledge bases
- Advanced customization options

**Milestones:**
- 50+ tokens collected
- Integrate with real-world API
- Create first custom integration
- PixlPal functions as personal assistant

#### Phase 4: Extension & Agentic Computing (Hours 12+)
- Endgame: PixlPal as functional personal assistant
- Community sharing of customizations
- Optional challenges and scenarios
- Foundation for lifelong learning

## User Experience Design Principles

### 1. Play Over Lectures
- No tutorial dialogs that explain concepts
- Learning happens through doing, discovering, and observing
- Failure is a teaching tool (bland PixlPal response teaches boundaries)

### 2. Transparency & Control
- Players always see what data PixlPal has
- Can manually override/edit prompts and tokens
- Understand that they're shaping the AI
- Foster agency and responsibility

### 3. Joy & Delight
- PixlPal is charming, responds thoughtfully to gifts
- Personality shines through interactions
- Visual/audio feedback for discoveries
- Celebration of milestones

### 4. Accessibility
- Works on mobile (primary platform)
- Settings for different age groups and learning styles
- Can be played casually or deeply depending on preference
- Optional challenges for depth seekers

### 5. Replayability
- Randomized personality traits per playthrough
- Multiple paths to discoveries
- Different token combinations create different experiences
- Community sharing encourages variation

- **Educator Adoption:** # of classrooms using PixlPal

## Technical Considerations

### Platform & Tech Stack
- **Primary Platform:** iOS and Android (React Native or Flutter)
- **Backend:** Cloud-based API for PixlPal interactions
- **AI Model:** Integration with LLM (likely GPT-4 or open-source alternative)
- **Data Storage:** User tokens, customizations, integration preferences
- **Privacy:** End-to-end encryption for user data, COPPA compliance

### Scalability
- Backend must handle peak concurrent users
- Token system must support real-time updates
- Integration framework must be extensible
- Analytics must track engagement and learning outcomes

### Safety & Content Moderation
- Content filter for harmful inputs/outputs
- Boundary enforcement system (bland responses to harassment)
- Privacy protection for minors (COPPA compliance)
- Transparent logging of interactions (for debugging and safety)
